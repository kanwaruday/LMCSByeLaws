Absence from School

  1. A student, returning to School after suffering an infectious or contagious disease, should produce a doctor's fitness certificate permitting her/him to attend School.
  2. Parents will be promptly informed in the event of serious illness or emergencies and may be requested to come to School to take their ward home if the case is serious enough to merit their attention.
  3. Repeated absence without leave or unexplained absence for more than six consecutive days renders the student liable to have her/his name struck off the rolls. Re-admission may only be granted after payment of a re-admission fee.
  4. All students are expected to attend School on the opening day immediately following the vacations. Absence for more than three days after the vacations end, will attract strict disciplinary action.
  5. It is compulsory for students to complete 90% attendance (80% in case of medical reasons) in each term in order to be eligible to take the end-of-the-term examination. This stipulation can only be relaxed on genuine medical grounds, at the sole discretion of the Head of the School.
