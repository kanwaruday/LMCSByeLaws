School Rules

  1. Every student must carry her/his Almanac to School every day and use it productively.
  2. Students who do not use school-arranged transport should ensure that they arrive at school at least 10 minutes before the first bell.
  3. The warning bell is rung 5 minutes before the start of the morning assembly.
  4. Students should move from one classroom to another between periods without wasting time, in an orderly and quiet manner.
  5. Students should be habitually clean and always neatly dressed.
  6. Non-Sikh boys should get their hair cut at regular intervals and ensure that it remains combed, short and tidy.
  7. No shouting or whistling is allowed in or around the School building. Running in corridors is strictly prohibited.
  8. It is strictly forbidden to throw any object at any one.
  9. Bullying and the use of foul language are punishable offences, whether in or out of School.
  10. Care must be taken of all School property and no student should scratch or spoil the desks or chairs, or damage any School furniture, or write or draw anything on the walls. Damage done, even by accident, should be reported at once to the School authorities.
  11. The cost of any wilful damage done will be made good by the concerned student, who will also be liable to pay a fine for any such offence.
  12. No irrelevant book (other than text books or library books), magazine or other publication may be brought to the School without the Class Teacher's explicit permission.
  13. Lending or borrowing of money or other articles is not permitted.
  14. Mobile phones, Audio and Video CDs, DVDs, Computer games are strictly banned in the School premises. Bringing these articles to School will lead to confiscation, never to be returned.
  15. Students who are escorted to and from School in the care of servants or domestic staff may not leave the School premises without their nominated escort.
  16. LMS students should always remember that the School is judged by their conduct outside the School premises, whether in or out of uniform.
  17. The School reserves the right to terminate the Schooling of students with unsatisfactory progress in studies or whose conduct is a bad precept for others.
  18. Exploding crackers or splashing colours during Diwali/Holi or any other occasion in the school premises or on School buses is strictly forbidden.
  19. Students must make every effort to be present for festivals and functions celebrated in the School which they are expected to attend.
  20. The School is committed to protect the environment and expects its students to be environment friendly in all their actions. Students should consciously avoid the use of polybags.
