Rewards & Punishments

🔴 Red Star

Careless and untidy work, indicating a general lack of effort in academics, may lead to the award of a Red Star by any teacher, which will be recorded on the Star Chart maintained in every classroom.

🔴 Red Card

  1. A student who gets 3 Red Stars, indicating a sustained lack of effort in academics, will be liable for the issue of a Red Card.
  2. The Head of the School, on the written recommendation of the Class Teacher(s), may issue a Red Card. This will be announced in Assembly and the name of the student will be posted on the School notice board.
  3. A student on a Red Card has to have the appropriate column in the card signed by the subject teacher concerned at the end of each period, on each day of the week. If progress during the week is satisfactory, the Head of School may discontinue the Red Card at the end of the week.

🟢 Green Star

A Green Star, recorded on the Star Chart, will be awarded to a student for exemplary behaviour and performance within the classroom and the School campus. Any subject teacher can bestow a Green Star. Certificates will be awarded to students accumulating the maximum Green Stars in their respective class.

🟡 Yellow Star

Yellow Stars may be given by Teachers for minor infringements of School rules and general misbehaviour. If a student gets 5 Yellow Stars she/he will be issued a Yellow Card.

🟡 Yellow Card

  1. Serious offences which adversely reflect a student's conduct — e.g., breaking norms, bullying, rudeness, insubordination, disobedience, cheating, telling lies — may lead to the issue of a Yellow Card.
  2. A Yellow Card is a serious matter. The offender is deprived of all privileges in the School for a period of one week. She/he is not allowed to attend any programme, picnic or outing while on a Yellow Card.
  3. A student on a Yellow Card must carry the card for the entire week's duration and is liable to have remarks entered on the card for any disciplinary infringement. At the end of the week, the student will meet the Head of the School.
  4. The receipt of two Yellow Cards within one academic year will lead to the expulsion of the offender from the School on disciplinary grounds.
