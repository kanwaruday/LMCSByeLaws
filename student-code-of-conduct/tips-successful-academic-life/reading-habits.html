Reading Habits & Its Nuances

Reading – The Most Precious Gift for Your Child

  1. Reading is the most important skill children ever learn in School and one best strengthened by the untiring efforts of enthusiastic parents.
  2. Learning to read takes time and effort. Reading is not something children can just pick up — children need encouragement to work at it and to keep trying if they get frustrated.
  3. Reading is the doorway to a lifetime of learning. Children learn the nuts and bolts at School, but they still need the special one-to-one attention only parents can provide. Reading together must be a top priority in your daily agenda.

Reading Tips

  1. Start today. The earlier, the better.
  2. Use the VAKT method: Visual, Audio, Kinaesthetic and Tactile — multi-sensory teaching techniques using all five sensory organs.
  3. Run your finger below the words as you read aloud.
  4. Show how you begin reading from the top and work down.
  5. Point out repetitive words. Ask children to spot these words.
  6. Talk about pictures. Unfamiliar words can be figured out by looking at pictures.
  7. Alternate story-reading sessions with days when your child narrates the story back to you.

Beyond Books

  1. Children should have opportunities to go places and do different things. The more they experience, the easier it is to read because of new ideas and vocabulary they are exposed to.
  2. Get children involved in everyday reading — directions, grocery lists, recipes, labels, instructions, road signs. Subscribe to children's newspapers.
  3. The ability to listen and not merely to hear is a prerequisite to developing holistically. Limit gadgets during the initial 3 planes of Education.
  4. Encourage children to pen down their thoughts. Keep pencil crayons and paper handy.

Approach to Reading

A balanced reading programme should employ phonics — teaching children how to sound out words. The four key language skills to develop:

  1. Listening Skill
  2. Speaking Skill
  3. Reading Skill
  4. Writing Skill
