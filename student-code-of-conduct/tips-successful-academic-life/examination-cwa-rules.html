Rules of Examination & CWA (Class Work Assessment)

Academic Year: The academic year will be divided into two semesters from March to February. Class Work Assessment will be conducted regularly. Report cards will be given to the parents at the end of the session.

Rules of Examination & CWA

  1. Attendance is compulsory for all tests and examinations held during the year, failing which the student will be given a zero. No consideration will be given to absentees, except on medical grounds, at the sole discretion of the Head of the School. It is the responsibility of both students and their parents to ensure that they do not miss any Written Tests or Examinations.
  2. A minimum attendance record of 90% of the total number of working days is compulsory. Even on medical grounds, a child remaining absent for more than 20% of the total number of working days in each term, may not be allowed to give examination.
  3. There is no provision for re-testing students, who remain absent on the day of the examination, or those who have failed in one or more subjects.
  4. Students reporting late for examinations will not be given any extra time for answering the question papers.
  5. Students found using any unfair means during any of the examinations, will be given zero in that subject. A repeat of such cheating in the future will result in the prompt issue of a Transfer Certificate, without notice.
  6. The answer scripts of the examinations will be distributed to students after evaluation. Errors in evaluation — such as mistakes in totalling, unmarked answers etc. — must be brought to the notice of the concerned Teachers instantly. Delaying this, may result in ignoring the same while preparing the final result.
  7. Requests for the issue of Progress Cards before the declaration of results will not be entertained.
