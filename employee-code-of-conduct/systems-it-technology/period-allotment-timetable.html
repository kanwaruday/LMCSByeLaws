Period Allotment & Time Table

This document is the ready-reckoner for day-to-day school operations. Staff are encouraged to keep this office order in their TPRs.

Guidelines for Period Allotment

  1. The Period Allotment sheet must be maintained in real-time on Google Sheets.
  2. The school follows a 9-period schedule.
  3. PGT / TGT / PRT Subject Teachers:
    • Each teacher working 6 hours/week can teach a maximum of 36 periods/week (6 periods per hour of daily work)
    • Objective: allocate 30–33 significant periods and 3–6 lighter periods
  4. PRT Mother Teachers:
    • Each teacher working 6 hours/week can teach a maximum of 42 periods/week (7 periods per hour)
    • Being a mother teacher, the teacher teaches all subjects
    • 2 periods allocated as checking period (free period) as table checking is a large part of the role
  5. MW (Montessori Wing) Teacher:
    • Most MW teachers teach for all 4 hours of their school time and are expected to stay with students throughout the day
    • If upgraded to a 6-hour school, the teacher is expected to take at least 2 out of 3 periods
  6. Ideally, each teacher should be assigned 4–5 classes. PTIs, Sanskrit, Drawing, Computer Teachers, and non-academic subject teachers may be assigned additional classes as required.
  7. Efforts should be made to assign class blocks of similar levels — avoid scenarios where a teacher teaches across vastly different grade levels (e.g., Class 3, 8, and 12).
  8. Subject combinations for classes and periods should be structured to facilitate smooth transition for new teachers.
  9. Teachers must receive at least one month's notice before any changes are implemented in period or class allotments.
  10. Where a teacher has more than 33 significant periods, substitution periods should not be allocated. Instead, assign substitution to the Principal, PTI, IT personnel, KKM staff, or non-teaching personnel involved in Smart Classes.

Guidelines for Timetable Setting

  1. A free period must be scheduled after every 3 consecutive periods; maximum 4 consecutive periods allowed.
  2. Games periods should commence only from the 3rd period or later.
  3. All class teachers must be present in their respective classes during the Lunch Break.
  4. An effort should be made to schedule all serious subjects before the 7th period.
  5. The distribution of periods per week must be evenly spread across each day.
  6. The timetable for the term should remain consistent; daily adjustments are recorded in adjustment registers based on teacher utilisation scores.
  7. New timetables MUST NOT be made. In case of an 8-period day, Period 9 is simply not taken.

Types of School Days

  • Normal Day: Standard daily schedule; Period 1 is 40 minutes to allow the class to settle
  • Zero Period Day: For school-level events with external attendees (Investiture, Annual Function, Sports Day)
  • Special Day with 2-Hour Event: For internal school-level events (Inter-House competitions, final rounds of school competitions)
  • Special Day with 1-Hour Event: For external guest visits (Alumni Interaction, talks, short events)
  • Inverted Zero Period: Only for special circumstances where morning practice is essential before a major event; only a small group, not the entire school, should use this time
  • Inverted Special Day (2hr/1hr): When an event cannot be organised in the afternoon
  • MW Normal Day / MW Zero Period / MW Inverted Zero Period: Corresponding schedules for the Montessori Wing
For the exact period-by-period timings for each day type, see Time Table Timings.
