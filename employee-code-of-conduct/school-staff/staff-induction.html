Staff Induction

All new staff members undergo a structured induction programme before commencing independent duties. The induction is coordinated by the Principal with support from MD Academics.

Job Roles Document

The induction begins with a detailed walkthrough of the official Job Roles document for the staff member's designation. The document covers:

  • Primary duties and daily responsibilities
  • Reporting structure and line management
  • Subject and class assignments (for teaching staff)
  • Administrative duties (registers, reports, school events)
  • Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) and evaluation criteria
  • Conduct expectations and communication protocols

A signed acknowledgement that the Job Roles document has been read and understood is filed in the staff dossier.

Salary Mechanism

Salary Components

Staff are briefed on the complete salary structure during induction:

  • Basic Salary: The fixed component as per the appointment letter.
  • HRA / Allowances: As applicable to the designation and branch.
  • Deductions: Including leave deductions, fines, and security deposits.

Leave Deductions

Staff are informed of the leave entitlement for their category and the per-day deduction rate for unauthorised or excess leave. Deductions are applied in the salary of the month in which the leave was taken or at the time of settlement.

Fines

Fines may be levied for: late arrival, early departure, failure to submit reports on time, violation of the dress code, or breach of conduct rules. Fine amounts are specified in the respective Office Orders and are communicated during induction.

Security Deposit

A refundable security deposit is deducted from the salary over the first few months of employment. The deposit amount depends on the designation. The deposit is refunded upon proper completion of notice period and handing-over formalities. It may be forfeited in cases of abrupt exit or misconduct.

Recruitment Requirement Form (RRF)

Staff are made aware that future internal transfers, role changes or grade changes are initiated through the RRF process and require prior approval from MD Academics.

Increments

Annual increments are performance-based and reviewed at the end of the academic year. The increment quantum is determined by:

  • Annual performance evaluation score
  • Attendance record for the year
  • Conduct and disciplinary record
  • MD Academics' recommendation

Increment letters are issued with the new academic year appointment. There is no automatic increment; all increments are discretionary.

Incentives

Staff are eligible for incentives as per the schemes active at the time of their joining. Incentive categories include:

  • Admission Incentive: For staff who generate confirmed student admissions.
  • Performance Bonus: For achieving academic or operational targets set at the start of the year.
  • Event Incentive: For exceptional contribution to school events or competitions.

Refer to the Incentives & Bonuses page for full details of active schemes.

Special Role Assignments

Some staff may be assigned special roles in addition to their primary designation. These include:

  • Class Teacher: Responsible for the overall welfare and academic tracking of an assigned class section.
  • Subject Coordinator: Responsible for curriculum planning, question paper review and subject-level reporting for a department.
  • Activity In-Charge: Responsible for organising and documenting a specific co-curricular activity or club.
  • Examination Coordinator: Responsible for question paper collection, seating arrangements and result compilation for examination sessions.

Special role assignments carry additional responsibilities and may attract additional allowances as notified by the management.
