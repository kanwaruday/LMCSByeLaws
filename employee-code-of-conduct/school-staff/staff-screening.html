Staff Screening

The LMCS hiring pipeline follows a structured 7-step screening process to ensure only qualified, suitable candidates are appointed to school positions.

Screening Pipeline

Step 1 — Application via Google Form

All candidates must fill the official LMCS hiring Google Form. Walk-in applications or informal referrals are not processed until the form is submitted. The form captures personal details, qualifications, teaching experience, subject specialisation and availability.

Step 2 — Initial Interview

Shortlisted candidates are called for an initial interview with the school Principal or HR Coordinator. The interview assesses communication skills, subject knowledge, attitude and cultural fit with the Montessori ethos. Candidates are evaluated on a standard scoring sheet.

Step 3 — Bio Data Submission

Candidates who clear the initial interview submit their full Bio Data form, which includes:

  • Personal information and contact details
  • Educational qualifications and copies of certificates
  • Previous employment history with reference contacts
  • Passport-size photograph
  • Government-issued ID proof

Step 4 — Written Test

A subject-specific written test is administered to assess academic competency. Teaching candidates take tests aligned to the subject and class level they will be teaching. Administrative candidates take tests on relevant administrative and communication skills.

Step 5 — Teacher Interview Questionnaire

Teaching candidates complete the LMCS Teacher Interview Questionnaire, which covers:

  • Pedagogical approach and classroom management philosophy
  • Familiarity with CBSE/HPBSE curriculum and assessment patterns
  • Ability to handle mixed-ability classrooms
  • Views on student discipline and parent communication
  • Scenario-based questions on handling academic and behavioural challenges

Step 6 — Interview Report

The interviewing Principal or HR Coordinator prepares a formal Interview Report summarising the candidate's performance across all steps. The report includes:

  • Scores from the written test
  • Ratings from the Teacher Interview Questionnaire
  • Interviewer's qualitative assessment and recommendation
  • Proposed designation, branch and salary range

The Interview Report is forwarded to MD Academics for final review.

Step 7 — MD Academics Approval & Talking Points

MD Academics reviews the Interview Report and Talking Points document prepared by the school. The Talking Points document outlines the candidate's strengths, any concerns, proposed salary and start date. MD Academics gives final approval before an offer is made. No appointment letter is issued without this approval.

Key Requirements

  • Teaching staff must hold a minimum of a Bachelor's degree in the relevant subject; B.Ed. is preferred.
  • Candidates for Class 1 and above must hold TET (Teacher Eligibility Test) qualification or be actively pursuing it.
  • Reference checks are conducted for all final candidates before the appointment letter is issued.
  • Background verification is mandatory for staff in positions involving direct student contact.
