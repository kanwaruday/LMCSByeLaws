Staff Exit

All staff separations — whether voluntary resignation, retirement or termination — must follow the formal exit procedure. No staff member is considered to have completed service until all exit formalities are completed.

Step 1 — Resignation Rules

A staff member wishing to resign must submit a written resignation letter to the Principal. The resignation must adhere to the following rules:

  • The notice period is as specified in the appointment letter (typically one month for teaching staff, two weeks for support staff).
  • Resignation must be submitted during a working day; email resignation is only valid if acknowledged in writing by the Principal.
  • Resignation during an examination period requires a minimum of one month's notice regardless of the appointment letter terms.
  • Immediate resignation (without notice) results in forfeiture of the security deposit and may result in deduction of salary in lieu of notice period.
  • The management reserves the right to accept or reject a resignation with immediate effect or to require the staff member to serve the full notice period.

Step 2 — Handing Over Form

Upon acceptance of the resignation, the staff member must complete the official Handing Over Form. This form documents:

  • Transfer of all school property (keys, equipment, books, stationery)
  • Completion of all pending reports and registers
  • Return of official ID card, school app login credentials and email access
  • Briefing of the incoming or replacement staff member on pending tasks
  • Clearance from library, inventory, finance and IT departments

The Handing Over Form must be countersigned by the Principal and a copy forwarded to Head Office HR.

Step 3 — No Objection Certificate (NOC)

Once all handing-over formalities are complete, the management issues a No Objection Certificate (NOC) to the staff member. The NOC confirms:

  • All dues have been cleared
  • No pending disciplinary proceedings
  • All school property has been returned
  • The management has no objection to the staff member joining another institution

The NOC is issued within 7 working days of completion of all formalities. The NOC is not issued if there are pending dues, unreturned property or ongoing disciplinary proceedings.

Step 4 — Full & Final Settlement

The Full & Final (F&F) Settlement is processed by the finance department after the NOC is issued. The settlement includes:

  • Salary for the last working month (pro-rated if applicable)
  • Leave encashment for any unused earned leave (as per applicable rules)
  • Refund of security deposit (if eligible as per resignation rules)
  • Deduction of any outstanding dues, fines or advance amounts

The F&F settlement amount is transferred to the staff member's registered bank account within 30 days of the last working day. A final settlement statement is provided to the staff member for their records.

Note: Staff who leave without completing the notice period or handing-over formalities are not eligible for the NOC or security deposit refund, and may be reported to relevant authorities in cases of abandonment of duty.
