Staff Operations

Day-to-day operational rules, conduct expectations, key performance indicators and performance evaluation procedures for all LMCS staff members.

School Rules

Code of Conduct

All staff are required to uphold the LMCS Employee Code of Conduct at all times on school premises, during school events and in all official communications. This includes:

  • Treating students, parents and colleagues with respect and professionalism
  • Maintaining confidentiality of student records and school information
  • Refraining from any form of corporal punishment or psychological abuse
  • Adhering to the dress code as prescribed for the branch and designation
  • Using only official channels for school communication

For full conduct rules, refer to School Rules.

Period Allotments

Teaching staff are assigned a weekly period allotment as part of the timetable. Period allotments are finalised by the Principal in consultation with the Timetable Coordinator at the beginning of each term. Staff must:

  • Be present in their assigned classroom before the bell rings for their period
  • Not leave the classroom unsupervised during a teaching period
  • Report any timetable conflict or scheduling issue to the Principal immediately
  • Not swap or reassign periods without written permission from the Principal

Timetabling

The school timetable is prepared at the start of each academic session. Changes to the timetable during the session must be approved by the Principal. Staff involved in preparing the timetable must ensure no teacher is assigned more than the permissible maximum periods per week as per HPBSE and CBSE norms.

Reporting

All teaching and administrative staff are required to submit periodic reports as assigned. This includes:

  • Daily Diary / Period Record (teaching staff)
  • Class Teacher Weekly Report
  • Teacher Report Register (TRR) entries
  • Monthly academic progress and activity reports

Reports must be submitted by the due date. Late submissions attract a fine as notified in the relevant Office Order.

Dress Code

Staff are required to wear formal attire suitable for an educational environment. Specific dress code requirements by designation and branch are as follows:

  • Teaching Staff (Female): Formal salwar suit or saree in muted, professional colours
  • Teaching Staff (Male): Formal shirt and trousers; tie on designated days
  • Administrative Staff: Formal office wear as per branch guidelines
  • PT / Sports Staff: Designated sports uniform as issued by the school

Jeans, t-shirts, casual footwear and heavy jewellery are not permitted on school days.

Communication

Staff communication with parents must be conducted via official channels (school diary, official WhatsApp group, email). Direct personal contact between staff and parents outside school channels is discouraged and must be reported to the Principal if it occurs. Internal communication between staff and management must use the official email ID.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

Each staff category has defined KPIs that are used for performance evaluation. Common KPIs include:

  • Attendance Rate: Target ≥ 95% of working days
  • Report Submission: All required reports submitted on time
  • Result Quality: Class average and pass percentage targets as set at the start of session
  • Student Feedback: Satisfactory ratings from periodic student and parent feedback
  • Professional Development: Participation in at least one training or workshop per year
  • Discipline Record: No formal disciplinary action during the year

KPIs are communicated to staff at the start of the academic year and reviewed quarterly by the Principal.

Performance Evaluation

Formal performance evaluations are conducted twice a year — mid-year (December) and end-of-year (March). The evaluation process involves:

  1. Self-assessment form filled by the staff member
  2. Principal's evaluation against the KPIs
  3. Classroom observation (for teaching staff) by the Principal or subject coordinator
  4. Feedback discussion between the Principal and staff member
  5. Final score submitted to MD Academics for increment and role-continuation decisions

Performance evaluation scores are confidential and stored in the staff personnel file. Staff have the right to request a review of their evaluation score through the Principal within 7 days of receiving the evaluation.
