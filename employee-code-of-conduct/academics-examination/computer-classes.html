Computer Subject: Project-Based Evaluation Policy

Computer Classes

This Office Order introduces a project-based evaluation system for the Computer Science subject, replacing the traditional written examination format. The new system emphasises practical skills and applied learning.

Evaluation System

Computer Science is evaluated through projects and practical work rather than written examinations. The grading system uses a letter-grade scale:

Grade Performance Level Description
AOutstandingExceptional project work demonstrating creativity, technical accuracy and independent thinking. Exceeds expectations in all evaluation criteria.
BProficientGood project work meeting all evaluation criteria with minor gaps. Demonstrates solid understanding of the concepts and practical skills.
CDevelopingSatisfactory work that meets basic requirements but lacks depth or accuracy in some areas. Concepts partially understood.
DNeeds ImprovementWork submitted does not meet minimum standards. Significant gaps in understanding or practical skills. Re-submission or remedial support required.

Project Requirements

  • Each student must complete the assigned computer project individually unless specified as a group project by the teacher.
  • Projects are assigned at the beginning of each term and must be submitted by the specified deadline. Late submissions attract a grade reduction.
  • Projects must be demonstrated live on the computer system; screenshots or printouts alone are not sufficient.
  • Plagiarised or copied projects receive a D grade automatically and the student is required to resubmit an original project.

Recording in TRR and CSM

Computer subject grades are recorded in two official systems:

  • TRR (Teacher Report Register): The Computer teacher records each student's project grade in the TRR under the "Computer Science" column. Grades are entered within 3 working days of project evaluation.
  • CSM (Class Summary Matrix / School Management System): The grade is entered into the school management system or the designated digital register by the Computer teacher or the Class Teacher, as instructed by the Principal.

Teacher Responsibilities

  • The Computer teacher must assign projects aligned to the NCERT/CBSE/HPBSE curriculum for the respective class.
  • Project rubrics must be shared with students at the time of assignment.
  • Evaluated projects must be returned to students with written feedback within 7 working days of submission.
  • The teacher must maintain a project record register noting the student name, class, project title, submission date and grade awarded.

Parent Communication

Computer project grades are communicated to parents through the school diary or the school management app at the end of each term along with other subject grades. Parents may request to view the evaluated project during the designated parent-teacher meeting.
