Science Project and Holiday Homework Guidelines 2024

Science Project & Holiday Homework 2024

This Office Order provides guidelines for the Science Project assigned as part of the 2024 Summer Holiday Homework. All Science teachers and Class Teachers must ensure students complete the project as specified below.

Project Categories

Students are assigned one of three project categories based on their class level:

Category Class Level Project Type
Category AClass III – Class VObservation-based science activity / simple working model
Category BClass VI – Class VIIIResearch Paper or Working Model (student's choice)
Category CClass IX – Class XResearch Paper or Working Model (student's choice)

Project Themes

Students may choose from any of the following 8 approved themes for their science project:

  1. Environment & Climate Change
  2. Human Body & Health
  3. Plants, Animals & Biodiversity
  4. Energy & Conservation
  5. Technology & Innovation
  6. Water & Natural Resources
  7. Space & the Universe
  8. Everyday Chemistry

Research Paper Format (Categories B & C)

Students choosing the Research Paper format must structure their work as follows:

  1. Title Page — Project title, student name, class, section and school name
  2. Introduction — Background and importance of the topic; research objective
  3. Materials & Methods — Data collection approach, sources used, experiments conducted
  4. Observations & Findings — Data, diagrams, photographs or charts
  5. Conclusion — Summary of findings and their significance
  6. Bibliography — List of books, websites and other references used

Research papers must be handwritten in the project notebook unless the teacher has specifically approved typed submissions.

Working Model Requirements (Categories B & C)

Students choosing the Working Model format must:

  • Build a functional model that demonstrates a scientific concept from the chosen theme
  • Prepare a one-page written explanation of the scientific principle demonstrated by the model
  • Be able to present and explain the model to the teacher and class
  • Label all components of the model clearly

Assessment Criteria

Projects are assessed on 5 parameters, each carrying equal weightage (20%):

Parameter Weightage What is Evaluated
Relevance & Theme Alignment20%How well the project addresses the chosen theme and scientific concepts
Originality & Creativity20%Evidence of independent thinking; not copied from textbooks or internet verbatim
Scientific Accuracy20%Correctness of scientific facts, terminology and conclusions drawn
Presentation Quality20%Neatness, organisation, labelling and visual clarity
Verbal Explanation20%Student's ability to explain the project clearly when asked

Submission & Evaluation

  • Projects must be submitted on the first day of school after the summer holidays.
  • Working models must be brought to school on the designated Science Project Presentation Day announced by the Principal.
  • Grades are awarded using the A/B/C/D system as per the Computer Classes evaluation policy.
  • Grades are recorded in the TRR under the "Science Project" column and entered into the CSM by the Science teacher.
  • Projects will not be accepted after the submission deadline without a prior written request to the Principal.
