External Assessment (EA) Implementation 2025–26

EA Implementation 2025

This Office Order introduces the implementation framework for External Assessments (EA) across all LMCS branches for the academic year 2025–26. EAs replace the previous DA/OD (Day Assessment / Online Assessment) format.

EA Cycles 2025–26

Five EA cycles are scheduled across the academic year:

Cycle Tentative Month Subjects Covered
EA 1May 2025English, Hindi, Maths, Science, Social Science
EA 2August 2025English, Hindi, Maths, Science, Social Science
EA 3October 2025English, Hindi, Maths, Science, Social Science
EA 4December 2025English, Hindi, Maths, Science, Social Science
EA 5February 2026English, Hindi, Maths, Science, Social Science

Subjects Conducted Online

The following 5 subjects are conducted in online mode for all EA cycles:

  1. English
  2. Hindi
  3. Mathematics
  4. Science / EVS
  5. Social Science / Environmental Studies

Blueprints by Class Level

Each class level has a specific blueprint governing marks distribution for EA. The blueprints are as follows:

M2 & M3 (Montessori 2 and 3)

  • Total Marks: 20 per subject per cycle
  • Format: Oral + Practical activity-based; no written EA for M2/M3
  • Assessment recorded by Class Teacher in TRR

Class I – II

  • Total Marks: 20 per subject per EA
  • Format: 10 Marks MCQ (Online) + 10 Marks Activity/Worksheet
  • Duration: 30 minutes online + 20 minutes worksheet

Class III – V

  • Total Marks: 25 per subject per EA
  • Format: 15 Marks MCQ (Online) + 10 Marks Short Answer
  • Duration: 40 minutes online + 20 minutes written

Class VI – VIII

  • Total Marks: 30 per subject per EA
  • Format: 20 Marks MCQ (Online) + 10 Marks Short Answer / Diagrams
  • Duration: 45 minutes online + 30 minutes written

Class IX – X

  • Total Marks: 40 per subject per EA
  • Format: 25 Marks MCQ (Online) + 15 Marks Short/Long Answer
  • Duration: 60 minutes online + 30 minutes written

Lab Capacity Requirement

Since EAs are conducted online, the following infrastructure requirements apply:

  • Each branch must have a functional computer lab with sufficient systems to accommodate at least one full class section simultaneously.
  • Minimum requirement: one computer/tablet per student for the EA session.
  • Branches that do not meet the lab capacity must report to MD Academics immediately so alternative arrangements (e.g., staggered batches) can be made.
  • Internet connectivity must be tested at least 24 hours before each EA cycle.
  • The Computer teacher / IT Coordinator is responsible for ensuring all systems are functional on EA day.

EA Scoring & Records

  • EA scores are automatically recorded in the school management system for online components.
  • Written component scores must be entered by the subject teacher within 3 working days of the EA date.
  • Cumulative EA scores replace the DA/OD columns in all report card formats from Academic Year 2025–26 onwards.
  • The EA schedule for each cycle is announced by MD Academics at least 2 weeks in advance.
