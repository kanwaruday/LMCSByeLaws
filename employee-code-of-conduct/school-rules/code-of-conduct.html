Employee Code of Conduct

Core Professional Commitment

Every teacher at La Montessori Schools shall:

  • Place the child's growth, dignity, safety, and learning at the centre of all professional decisions.
  • Support the holistic development of each student — academic, emotional, social, moral, physical and life-skill development.
  • Contribute to a culture of excellence, discipline, care and continuous improvement consistent with the institution's stated vision.
  • Prepare students not only for examinations, but also for the challenges of the future through values, confidence, communication and practical competence.

Conduct with Students

Teachers shall:

  • Treat every student with respect, patience, fairness and compassion, without discrimination on the basis of gender, religion, caste, language, ability, family background or academic performance.
  • Maintain a child-friendly, safe and encouraging classroom environment that promotes curiosity, participation and confidence.
  • Use positive discipline at all times. Humiliation, intimidation, sarcasm, abusive language, physical punishment or emotional harassment are strictly prohibited.
  • Protect student confidentiality and handle personal, academic and behavioural information responsibly.
  • Be attentive to student wellbeing and promptly report concerns relating to safety, abuse, neglect, bullying or mental distress through school-approved channels.
  • Encourage student participation in co-curricular, sports, cultural, assembly and community-oriented activities.

Teaching and Learning Standards

Teachers shall:

  • Plan and deliver teaching that is child-centric, inclusive, engaging and age-appropriate, in line with the school's educational philosophy.
  • Integrate life skills, values, communication, collaboration and problem-solving into classroom practice wherever possible.
  • Maintain high academic expectations while providing support for varied learning needs.
  • Assess students honestly, fairly and transparently, and avoid favouritism or manipulation of marks, records or feedback.
  • Keep classroom records, lesson plans, student work and assessment data accurate and up to date.
  • Continuously improve subject knowledge, pedagogy, digital competence and classroom practice through reflection, training and collaboration.

Professional Behaviour

Teachers shall:

  • Demonstrate punctuality, preparedness and regular attendance in all school duties including classes, assemblies, meetings, examinations, supervision, events and parent interactions.
  • Dress and behave in a manner that reflects professionalism, modesty, self-respect and the reputation of La Montessori Schools.
  • Use respectful language and maintain self-control in all interactions, including during conflict or stress.
  • Refrain from gossip, defamation, harassment, bullying, intimidation or conduct that creates division among staff, students or parents.
  • Avoid any behaviour, online or offline, that may damage the reputation, trust or educational environment of the school.
  • Not engage in any form of corruption, bribery, exploitation, coercion or misuse of authority.

Conduct with Colleagues and Leadership

Teachers shall:

  • Work cooperatively with colleagues across campuses and departments in the shared interest of students and the institution.
  • Respect the roles of school leaders, coordinators, support staff and administrative teams.
  • Share professional knowledge, teaching resources and constructive feedback in a spirit of teamwork.
  • Raise concerns through proper channels respectfully and professionally.
  • Never undermine colleagues, spread misinformation or create hostility in the workplace.

Conduct with Parents and Community

Teachers shall:

  • Communicate with parents courteously, honestly and constructively.
  • Present student progress accurately and sensitively, with a focus on solutions and development.
  • Avoid making false, misleading, inflammatory or unauthorised public statements about the school, students, parents or colleagues.
  • Support school events, public functions and community activities in ways that uphold the institution's image and values.

Digital and Social Media Conduct

Teachers shall:

  • Use school digital platforms, messaging groups, email, ERP systems and social media responsibly and professionally.
  • Protect confidential school information, student images, parent data, assessment material and internal communications from unauthorised sharing.
  • Not post content that is disrespectful, political in a divisive sense, abusive, obscene or harmful to the school's reputation.
  • Ensure that any online engagement related to students or school activities is appropriate, transparent and authorised.

Safety, Inclusion and Student Protection

Teachers shall:

  • Follow all school rules and legal requirements relating to child protection, safety, supervision, emergency response, transport, excursions and campus discipline.
  • Immediately report misconduct, safety hazards, suspected abuse, discrimination or serious policy violations.
  • Create inclusive classrooms where each child feels seen, heard and encouraged.
  • Be especially sensitive to younger children, vulnerable students and those with different learning or emotional needs.

Integrity and Conflict of Interest

Teachers shall:

  • Act honestly in all academic, financial, administrative and interpersonal matters.
  • Not accept or solicit undue favours, gifts, money or benefits in exchange for academic advantage, admissions influence or special treatment.
  • Not provide paid private tuition to currently enrolled students where prohibited by school policy or where it creates a conflict of interest.
  • Use school property, materials, time and institutional resources only for legitimate educational and professional purposes.
