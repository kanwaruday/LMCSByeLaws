Refundable Reserve Fund (RRF) & Security

Refundable Reserve Fund (RRF)

  • The RRF is a deduction from the employee's salary that the School holds as a refundable security fund.
  • Applies to employees who joined on or after 1 January 2020.
  • The RRF is intended to be equal to 3 months of an employee's CTI.
  • The RRF is deducted over 3 years at diminishing rates:
    Period Since DOJRRF Deduction Rate
    Up to 12 months12%
    13 – 24 months9%
    25 – 36 months6%
    After 36 months0%
  • The employee is expected to apply for a refund of the RRF after being relieved of School duties. The RRF will be refunded within 3 months of the application date.
  • In the event of lapses in adherence to the rules, the School reserves the right to make suitable monetary deductions. However, any remaining balance shall still be payable.
  • If any dues are pending at the time of leaving, the School will deduct these from the RRF.

RRF as a Loan

  • Employees may borrow up to 70% of the total accumulated RRF as an interest-free short-term loan.
  • The borrowed amount must be repaid before the end of each financial year.
  • An employee may take an employee loan a maximum of once every 2 years.
  • The loan may be repaid in instalments deducted from the employee's monthly salary.

Security Deposit

  • The Security is an amount deducted from the salary of an employee, retained by the School in case of non-compliance with resignation rules.
  • The balance due at the time of resignation is refundable subject to complete adherence to all resignation rules and applicable deductions.
  • The employee may apply for refund after relieving from duties; the Security will be refunded within 3 months from the date of application.
  • Discontinued for employees joining after 31 December 2019. Employees who joined before this date will be part of the RRF Scheme.
  • In case of any pending dues at the time of leaving, the School shall deduct the same from the Security Fund. The balance shall still be payable.
