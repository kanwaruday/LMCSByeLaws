Resignation Rules

Before accepting an employee's resignation from the post held, the following must be verified:

  1. The resignation is not submitted in the middle of the academic session, as prescribed by the affiliating board.
  2. Written notice (email or application) regarding the resignation has been submitted to the Head of the Institution.
  3. The resignation must be submitted before the 10th of the month; otherwise, it will be considered for the following month.
  4. For an employee who has served for more than 11 months at the institution, three working months' notice (90 days) or salary equivalent to the notice period must be paid.
  5. For an employee who has worked for less than 12 months at the institution, one working month's notice (30 days) or salary equivalent must be paid.
  6. If the resignation is submitted before completion of three years of service, it must be verified whether the employee has been paid salary for the vacation period and, if so, whether the salary drawn for the immediately preceding vacation period is to be refunded.
  7. A NOC (No Objection Certificate) has been obtained from the relevant departments/sections (TPR, Resource Books, Student Records, Salary Account, Student Money Accounts, and any other) of the Institution.
  8. If the above conditions are fulfilled, the Head of the Institution shall forward the resignation letter to the Governing Body with suitable opinion/remarks for orders/acceptance. The Management has the right to reject the resignation in the interest of the students.
  9. After receiving the orders/acceptance, the same shall be communicated to the employee by the Head of the Institution concerned.
  10. The salary for the month in which the resignation is submitted, and thereafter, shall not be drawn until the Management accepts the resignation.
  11. The Governing Body shall be the competent authority to accept the resignation of all employees.
  12. If an employee gives notice of resignation but withdraws it before the end of the notice period, only the date of joining changes; all privileges, perks and salaries remain the same.
  13. If an employee resigns and is off service for more than a week, they shall be treated as a fresh recruit and shall restart the probation period.
