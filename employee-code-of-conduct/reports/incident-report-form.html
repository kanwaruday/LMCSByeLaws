Student Incident Report Form

Incident Report Form

The Student Incident Report is a structured document used to record and address various incidents involving students. It ensures all relevant details are captured systematically, promoting transparency, accountability, and appropriate resolution.

Why File an Incident Report?

  • Parent may get involved — situations requiring parental involvement require thorough documentation.
  • Parent may question later — parents may enquire or raise accusations about incidents retrospectively.
  • Outside individuals are involved — when non-school individuals are part of the incident.
  • Escalation beyond the Principal — serious cases that need escalation to higher authorities.

When to File an Incident Report

  • Student injuries / medical emergencies
  • Student fights / violence
  • External complaints
  • Feedback from parents
  • Major damage at school
  • Substance abuse
  • Theft cases
  • Behavioural issues
  • Misuse of technology
  • Chronic absenteeism (more than 1 week)
  • Academic deviation
  • Sexual assault
  • Corporal punishment

How to Fill the Incident Report Form

1. Student Information

  • Collect and record the student's name
  • Note the student's class

2. Incident Details

  • Record the date and time the incident occurred
  • Specify the location (e.g. class period, subject)
  • Provide a detailed description including the sequence of events and direct quotes if relevant

3. Summary by Individuals Involved & Witnesses

  • Statement by the student/group involved (one side)
  • Statement by the student/group involved (other side)
  • Statement by a neutral student witness (preferably a school appointee)
  • Statement by the teacher who witnessed the incident
  • Statement by the helping staff who witnessed the incident

4. Student Background

Ask the student the following to gauge their background:

  • Parents'/guardians' occupations and time of reaching home
  • Student's daily routine
  • Their 2 best friends at school
  • What the student does after school
  • Form an opinion about their background based on the above

5. Academic & Behavioural Feedback

  • Take feedback from all teachers
  • The Class Teacher must include the following scores:
    • Class Discipline (CD)
    • Class Response (CR)
    • Homework (HW)
    • Class Work (CW)
    • CWA average score for the ongoing semester

6. Action Taken

  • Document any immediate actions taken at the time (medical attention, separation of students, notification of authorities)

7. Follow-Up Actions

  • Actions taken by the teacher after the incident
  • Actions taken by the student after the incident
  • Actions taken by the parents after the incident
  • Any other relevant remarks or observations

8. Principal Information

  • The Principal signs and dates the document to confirm completion and review
