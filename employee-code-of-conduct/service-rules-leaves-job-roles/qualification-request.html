Qualification Requirements for Teaching Staff

Qualification Requirements

This Office Order outlines the qualification requirements and related directives for all teaching staff employed at La Montessori Chain of Schools. All existing and newly appointed teaching staff must comply with the requirements outlined below.

1. Educational Qualifications Update

All teaching staff are required to ensure their educational qualifications are current and relevant to their assigned subject and class level. Specifically:

  • Teaching staff who do not hold a B.Ed. degree are strongly encouraged to pursue the qualification. B.Ed. is a preferred qualification for all teaching positions.
  • Staff currently enrolled in B.Ed. or equivalent teacher training programmes must inform the Principal and provide proof of enrolment.
  • Updated qualification certificates must be submitted to the school within 30 days of obtaining them, for inclusion in the staff personnel file and Head Office HR records.
  • Failure to update qualification records may affect performance evaluation scores and increment decisions.

2. TET (Teacher Eligibility Test) Essentiality

In accordance with the Right to Education (RTE) Act and HP government guidelines, TET qualification is mandatory for teaching staff assigned to Classes I through VIII. The following rules apply:

  • All staff teaching Classes I–V must hold or be actively pursuing HPTET (HP State TET) or CTET (Central Board TET) Paper I.
  • All staff teaching Classes VI–VIII must hold or be actively pursuing HPTET or CTET Paper II.
  • Existing staff who do not hold TET must register for the upcoming TET examination. Proof of registration must be submitted to the Principal.
  • Staff appointed to teach Classes I–VIII without TET qualification are employed on a conditional basis and must obtain TET within the time frame specified by the management.
  • Non-compliance with TET requirements may result in reassignment to non-TET-required roles or separation from service.

3. Self-Made Question Papers

All teaching staff are required to prepare their own question papers for school examinations. The following rules apply:

  • Question papers must be prepared by the subject teacher assigned to the class, not sourced from external agencies or copied from previous years without modification.
  • Question papers must comply with the LMCS blueprint and examination process guidelines. Refer to the Blueprints 2024 page for subject-wise marks distribution.
  • Question papers must be submitted to the Principal for review at least 5 working days before the examination date.
  • Failure to submit original, self-made question papers on time will be treated as a dereliction of duty and may attract a fine.

4. Use of Official Email IDs Only

All official correspondence related to qualifications, TET registration, training programmes and question paper submission must be conducted using the official LMCS email ID assigned to the staff member. Specifically:

  • Qualification-related documents submitted to the management must be sent from the official email ID only.
  • Correspondence with the HP Education Department, CBSE or any examination board regarding school examinations must be routed through the official email ID.
  • Personal email IDs must not be used for any official communication. Use of personal email for official purposes may result in the communication being disregarded and disciplinary action being initiated.
