System of Increments

Fixed Annual Increment

  • Salary increments are made with the salary of September every year, payable in October.
  • Increments are made as per the rules and regulations of Government Institutions — typically 3% annual increment.

Increment Due to Changes in Dearness Allowance (DA)

  • A change in the percentage of Dearness Allowance may also affect the net pay.
  • DA changes are decided by the School Management based on the financial performance and viability of the schools.
  • DA changes are usually implemented with the first month's salary of the new academic year.

Changes in Increment Criteria w.e.f. 1st January 2020

  • With effect from 1st January 2020, at the time of joining, each new employee starts with 3 increments already applied to their base salary structure.
  • New employees are subject to no further increments in the total Cost to Institution for the first 3 years from the date of joining.
  • However, employees will experience an annual increase in Net Payable Salary during this period due to the diminishing percentages of the RRF (Refundable Reserve Fund) deduction each year.

Increment Eligibility Rule

Condition at time of September Increment Increment Applied
(Salary Date − Date of Joining) > 36 months Increment + 1 (annual increment granted)
(Salary Date − Date of Joining) ≤ 36 months Increment + 0 (no increment this year)
The 3-year lock-in applies only to increases in the total Cost to Institution. The employee's take-home pay still increases each year as the RRF deduction percentage reduces (12% → 9% → 6% → 0% after 3 years).
