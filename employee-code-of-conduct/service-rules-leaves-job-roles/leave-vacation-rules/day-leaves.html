Day Leaves

Day-based leave categories cover full-day absences. Each has different approval requirements and salary implications.

Single Leave (Unpaid)

A Single Leave is one unpaid working day off. It is granted when an employee has exhausted their Casual Leave balance for the year.

  • Treated as an unpaid day — one day's salary is deducted.
  • Requires prior written application and Principal's approval.
  • If the leave is sandwiched around a public holiday (i.e., taken the day before or after a Sunday/gazetted holiday), the holiday is also counted as a Single Leave (sandwich rule). The sandwiched day becomes unpaid as well.

Double Leave

A Double Leave counts as two leave days for one calendar day's absence. It applies in specific circumstances:

  • Maximum ⌊(total teachers at branch ÷ 10)⌋ Double Leaves may be active simultaneously at a single branch on any given day. If the limit is reached, additional applications will be deferred.
  • Consecutive absences exceeding 6 working days trigger automatic classification as a Long Leave, which requires MD Operations approval and may affect service continuity.

Absent

An employee is marked "Absent" when they fail to report to duty without prior approval and without notifying the school.

Rule Details
Leave equivalent 1 Absent = 2 unpaid leave days deducted from salary
Notification deadline Employee must notify the Principal by 6:00 PM the previous evening if they cannot attend the next day. Failure to notify = Absent (not Single Leave)
Break in service 7 or more consecutive Absent days without communication = break in service. The employee may be treated as having abandoned their post, and disciplinary proceedings will be initiated
Employees who are marked Absent repeatedly (more than 3 times in a term) will be counselled by the Principal. A fourth instance in the same term may result in a formal show-cause notice.
