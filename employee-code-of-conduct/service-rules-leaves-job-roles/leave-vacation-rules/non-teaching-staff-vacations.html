Subject: Vacation Period — Leave Treatment for Non-Teaching Staff

Non-Teaching Staff Vacations

Administrative staff, helping staff, fee clerks, and other non-teaching employees are expected to work during periods when teaching staff are on vacation. This office order defines how their Casual Leave accrues during such periods.

The Rule

For every 2 working days that a non-teaching employee works during a vacation period, they accrue 1 Casual Leave (CL).

In other words: ½ of the working days in the vacation period = CL granted.

Worked Example

Consider a vacation period of 26 calendar days:

Item Count
Total vacation calendar days 26
Less: Sundays and Saturdays (off days) −5
Less: Gazetted holidays within the period −2
Working days (non-teaching staff report) 19
CL accrued (½ of 19, rounded down) 9.5 CL

Important Notes

  • This accrual is in addition to the standard 1 CL/month entitlement for non-teaching staff.
  • CL accrued under this order is subject to the same claim deadline (31 March) and no-carry-forward rules as regular CL.
  • Non-teaching staff who are absent during a vacation working day will have that day deducted from their accrued CL for that period.
  • The Admin Manager calculates and communicates the CL credit to each non-teaching employee at the end of each vacation period.
