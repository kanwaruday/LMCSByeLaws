General Leave Rules

The following 11 rules apply to all employees across all LMCS branches, regardless of leave category.

  1. Leave is a privilege, not a right. All leave must be applied for and approved in advance. Unapproved absence is treated as "Absent" and carries a double-leave deduction.
  2. First-come, first-served. When two employees apply for the same date, the earlier application takes precedence. No two staff members in the same role at the same branch may be on leave simultaneously without prior approval from the MD Operations.
  3. One-day advance written notice required. Leave applications must be submitted to the Principal at least one working day before the intended leave date. Emergency leaves must be communicated by 6:00 PM the previous day (see Absent rules).
  4. Responsibility handover is mandatory. Before proceeding on leave, the employee must hand over all pending duties, registers, and responsibilities to a designated colleague. Failure to do so may result in leave being treated as unauthorised.
  5. External remuneration = marked on leave. Any employee who takes on paid work outside LMCS (private tuition, event hosting, freelance assignments, etc.) on a working day will be marked on leave for that day, regardless of physical presence at school.
  6. WOH 2-hour absence = half day. An employee absent for 2 or more hours during the Work-On-Hours (WOH) period — without prior approval — is marked as a half-day leave.
  7. Sanctioned leave does not guarantee pay. Whether leave is paid depends on the leave category (CL = paid; Unpaid Leave = unpaid). The employee must confirm the leave type with the Admin Manager before proceeding.
  8. Leave lapses on resignation. Any unused Casual Leave balance lapses on the date of resignation or termination. There is no encashment of CL at the time of leaving service.
  9. Leave during the first 3 months of service. Employees in their probationary period (first 3 months) are not entitled to Casual Leave. Any absence during this period is treated as Unpaid Leave.
  10. Sandwiched leaves. If a leave is taken on a day immediately before or after a holiday (Sunday, gazetted holiday, or school vacation), the intervening holiday is also counted as leave unless specifically exempted by the MD Operations.
  11. Leave records are maintained centrally. The Admin Manager maintains the master leave register. All branch Principals must report leaves by the end of each working day to ensure accurate records.
