Teacher

General Job Roles

  • Act as a friend, guide, mentor, motivator, facilitator, preacher, and role model to students.
  • Teach and facilitate learning among students. Be receptive to queries and clear them to the best of your abilities.
  • Assess student progress through quizzes, tasks, projects, assessments, written and oral tests and assignments. Maintain a softcopy of marks on the School Software and a hardcopy record in the TPR.
  • Dedicate a week or more for revision of the syllabus before each terminal exam.
  • Communicate in the School only in English. Guide children on proper pronunciation and language skills.
  • Maintain attendance for respective classes.
  • Prepare Blueprint, Paper Setting, Marking Scheme and Solutions for examination papers. Undertake examination duty, followed by Checking, Super-Checking and marks updation on the School Management Software.
  • Stay up to date on new trends and changes in the education sector through workshops, seminars, self-research, journals, and websites.
  • Use audio-visual aids like Digital Classrooms to make learning a fun-filled experience.
  • Ensure discipline within school premises.
  • Prepare children to be future model citizens of the country.
  • Educate students on etiquettes, health, and personal hygiene especially during lunch break.
  • Impart student-centric education. Include students in remedial classes, especially under-achievers. Counsel students at various points of schooling.
  • Organise outdoor activities like field trips, picnics, and games with an aim of providing holistic and practical education.
  • Prepare students and fellow teachers for school events like Annual Functions, Investiture Ceremonies, Sports Days, and Morning Assemblies.
  • Take classes (periods) as per the requirement of the school in the best interest of students.
  • Hold various positions of responsibility like house moderator, activity in-charge, etc.
  • Formulate a lesson plan and execute it as per CBSE curriculum while incorporating activities to challenge students' creativity.
  • Maintain a TPR (Teacher's Personal Record) in detail.
  • Update marks on the School Management Software along with a record in the TPR.
  • Work as a good link between students, management, and parents. Maintain cordial relations with parents, fellow faculty, and management.
  • Inform and guide parents about the progress of their children.

Language Teachers

  • Give special emphasis to the pronunciation of spoken language.
  • Practise and evaluate Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking skills in the classroom.
  • Work on students' writing skills; prepare appropriate content for the School Blog or magazine.
  • (Sanskrit Teacher specific) Ensure correct Mantra Ucharna (chanting) pronunciation.

Science Teacher

  • Conduct teaching more by practicals than by textbooks; give hands-on experience alongside text-book teaching.
  • It is recommended that Science is taught without the use of a physical textbook in class.
  • Identify, help, and prepare students for various Science-based competitions and exhibitions.
  • Maintain the respective Science Laboratory and keep an account of the inventory.
  • Conduct Science Practicals as per the prescribed curriculum.
