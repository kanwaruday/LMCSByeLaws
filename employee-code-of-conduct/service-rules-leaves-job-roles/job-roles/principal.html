Principal

Core Responsibilities

  1. Supervise, guide, and control the work of teaching and non-teaching staff of the school.
  2. Carry out all administrative duties required of a head of office.
  3. Be responsible for the proper maintenance of school records, service records of teachers, and other registers.
  4. Oversee money-related handovers to the accounts in-charges.
  5. Handle official correspondence and furnish, within specified dates, returns and information required by the State Government / Board.
  6. Ensure that fines and misc. charges are realised and appropriately accounted for and duly appropriated for the purpose for which they were levied.
  7. Conduct physical verification of school property and stock at the beginning, end of the first semester, and at the end of the year — hence 3 times a year.
  8. Make satisfactory arrangements for good drinking water and other facilities for students.
  9. Give appropriate suggestions to the Coordinator to ensure that the school building, fixtures, furniture, office equipment, lavatories, playgrounds, and school garden are properly maintained.
  10. Be in-charge of admissions, preparation of school timetable, allocation of duties and teaching load to teachers, and provide necessary facilities to teachers in the discharge of their duties.
  11. Plan the year's academic work in advance in consultation with colleagues. Hold staff meetings at least once a month, review work done, and assess student progress.
  12. Help and guide teachers to promote their professional growth. Actively encourage participation in in-service education.
  13. Promote teachers' initiative for self-improvement and encourage educationally sound experiments.
  14. Supervise classroom teaching and secure co-operation among teachers of the same subject as well as inter-subject coordination.
  15. Arrange for special remedial teaching for children who need it.
  16. Plan and specify a regular timetable for scrutiny of pupils' written work and homework. Ensure timely and effective assessment and correction.
  17. Organise and coordinate various co-curricular activities through the house system.
  18. Send regularly the progress reports of students to their parents or guardians by means of Monthly Open Houses (MOH).
  19. Promote the physical well-being of pupils; ensure high standards of cleanliness and health habits.
  20. Devote at least 18 periods per week to teaching.
