School Appointee Selection — Investiture Ceremony 2024

Investiture Ceremony 2024

Appointments for this year's School Appointees. Selection competitions commence 3rd April 2024. All teachers are expected to impart necessary skills to students.

Positions Open for Competition

  • Two Student Representatives for LMS 2 (Class 12)
  • Sports Captain (Class 9/10)
  • Editorial Captain (Class 9/10)
  • Band Leader for LMS 2 (Class 9/10)
  • Captain – ERST Houses (Class 10 & Class 6)
  • Vice Captain – ERST Houses (Class 9 & Class 5)

Selection Procedure

  • Student Representative: Topper of all competitions.
  • Editorial Captain: Top 5 in Declamation → top scorer of all competitions.
  • Sports Captain: Top 5 in Physical Test → top scorer of all competitions.
  • House Captains: Top 4 scorers of Classes 10 & 6 (after Student Rep, Editorial and Sports Captain are selected).
  • House Vice Captains: Top 4 scorers of Classes 9 & 5.

Resume Making Guidelines

Teachers Responsible: Class Teachers (non-IC Classes 3, 4, 7 & 8); English Teachers (IC Classes 5, 6, 9, 10 & 12)

Primary Wing: Name & contact, About Me, School details, Skills, Wins & Responsibilities, Fun activities — neat and colourful with no spelling mistakes.

Senior Wing: Header, Objective, Education, Skills, Achievements & Awards, Volunteer Experience, Interests & Hobbies — clean layout, concise, proofread.

Assessment (out of 30): Presentation 10 pts | Academic Achievements 10 pts | Extra-Curricular Achievements 10 pts

Written Ability Test (Class 7–12)

Conducted by: System Coordinator with IT Teachers. Tests IQ across: Verbal Reasoning, Mathematical Reasoning, Logical Reasoning, Visual-Spatial Reasoning and Abstract Reasoning. Assessment based on absolute score.

Manifesto Guidelines

Teachers Responsible: Class Teachers (non-IC); Social Science Teachers (IC Classes)

A campaign poster including: Name & Position, Vision Statement, Promises, Personal Touch and Call to Action. Assessment (out of 30): Clarity of Message 10 pts | Originality & Visual Appeal 10 pts | Alignment with School Values 10 pts

Declamation Guidelines

Timing: 2 minutes up to Class 6; 3 minutes Class 7 and above. Focus on vocal delivery, emotional connection, eye contact, confidence and a strong ending.

Assessment (out of 30): Delivery, Confidence & Presence 10 pts | Relevance of Content 10 pts | Alignment with School Values 10 pts

Day 1: Class-wise selection (1 pm–3 pm) — Class Teacher/Subject Teacher picks best candidates per class.

Day 2: School-level competition (1 pm–3 pm) — 3 judges; simultaneous two-location format if space allows.

Election

Junior (Class 3–6): 2 ballot boxes per class; students cast 4 votes per box.

Senior (Class 7–12): Google Form using school email IDs; maximum 4 votes per post; 1 vote per email.

Point allocation: 1st most voted = 10 pts | 2nd = 8 pts | 3rd = 6 pts | 4th = 4 pts | Others = 0 pts

Physical Test (Class 7–12)

Conducted by PTIs. Three components:

  • Endurance Test: 10-minute jog (1 pt per minute)
  • Strength Test: Push-ups in 30 seconds (Boys: 25+ = 10 pts; Girls: 10+ = 10 pts, scaled down)
  • Balance Test: Stork Stand (stand on 1 foot — longest = 10 pts, scaled)

Personal Interview (Class 7–12)

Panel: House Moderators + Principal + MDs (optional).

Assessment (out of 30): Communication Skills & Confidence 10 pts | Leadership Potential 10 pts | Etiquette & Overall Impression 10 pts

Report of Interview (Class 7–12)

The candidate writes a self-appraisal report covering: each interview question and their answer, their strengths, areas for improvement and lessons learned.

Assessment (out of 30): Accuracy & Relevance 10 pts | Analysis of own Interview 10 pts | Presentation & Formatting 10 pts
