Student Timings

  • 5–11 July (Examination): 9 am to 1 pm
  • 12–13 July: 10 am to 1 pm (only participating/organising students)
  • Students organising the School Fete could be present on 12–13 July under the guidance of a Fete In-Charge.
  • Students must also be called for a review of Science Project Ideas on 12–13 July.

Teacher Timings

  • 5–11 July: 9 am to 3 pm (Examination days)
  • 12–13 July: 10 am to 3 pm (Result Preparation days)
  • Teachers can be allowed to leave at 1 pm until 11 July — Principal must approve; teachers must provide assurance of speedy & accurate answer sheet checking.
  • It is mandatory for all teachers to be on full-day mode (10 am to 3 pm) on 12 & 13 July.

Result Preparation — Items to be Completed by Evening of 13 July

  • Complete Checking
  • Complete Super Checking
  • Rectification sheets approved by Principals
  • Term I final result sheet
  • CSM updates: Marks, Grades, Remarks (after Principal approval), Student awards & achievements, Competition participations, Certificate particulars, Co-Scholastic Marks, Height & Weight

Principal Follow-up (1–3 pm daily during exams)

  • Start Report Card checking class-wise and student-wise even during exams.
  • Random answer checking of teachers to assess quality of checking.
  • Rectification sheet of each checked bundle so work doesn't pile up.
  • Supervision of QP Printing for next day.

Combined Teachers' Meeting — 29 July

Broad agendas (final list to be shared 1 week prior):

  1. New good practices for Term 2
  2. Course Mapping for Term 2 — sync LMS 1 & 4 and LMS 2 & 3; use week numbers for CWA dates
  3. Showcase Blueprints for Term 2
  4. Annual Function Dress Order
  5. Performances finalisation for Annual Function (as per OO/14/24)
  6. Sound check and Backdrop ideation for Annual Function
  7. Annual Report Review
  8. CSM Mobile Application training & implementation
  9. Discussion on Weekly Teachers' Meeting
  10. Implementation of Embibe across Schools

Open House & School Fete — 30–31 July

30 July — Preparation Day: Dedicated for preparation of the School Fete as an Inter House Competition. 5 stalls as follows:

  • 1 stall each for the ERST Houses (managed by House Captains & Vice Captains with Moderators)
  • 1 stall managed by the School Diamonds (with help from other teachers)

Stall in-charges fill a simple format declaring turnover, costings, expenses and profit. Winner = stall with the most profit, teaching students the essence of entrepreneurship.

Profit rules: Must be invested in the School itself; investment decided by School Appointees in consultation with the Principal; must be for the long-term benefit of students.

31 July — Open House & School Fete.
