Subject: Inter-School Science Competition — Guidelines

LMS Science Competition

The Inter-School Science Competition includes three activities: a Design Thinking Workshop by the Managing Director (Operations), a Science Quiz, and a Science Projects & Research Papers Competition.

I. Design Thinking Session

One-hour workshop led by the Managing Director of Operations. All participants attend.

II. Science Quiz

Participants: Each school nominates a team of 2 students divided into two categories:

  • Category I (Classes 6–8): 6 rounds
  • Category II (Classes 9–10): 8 rounds

Quiz Rounds

Round Category I (6–8) Category II (9–10)
General Science✅✅
Physics & Chemistry✅✅
Biology & Environmental Science✅✅
Technology & Innovations✅✅
Audio/Visual✅✅
Inventions & Discoveries✅✅
Riddle/Puzzles❌✅
Space❌✅

Quiz Rules

  • Each round has 4 questions or tasks
  • 15 seconds to answer each question; 20 seconds for audio/visual and riddle/puzzle rounds
  • 10 points for correct answer; 5 points for correctly answering a passed question; no negative marking
  • Tie-breaker: 5 buzzer questions; team pressing first answers within 10 seconds; correct = 10 points
  • Sudden death question if still tied after buzzer round
Quiz Master: PW Science Teachers (teachers of participating classes are excluded). Each science teacher must submit questionnaires via the Google Form shared by the System Coordinator.

III. Science Projects & Research Papers

Participants: Two individual teams per school for Science Projects; one team for Research Papers.

  • Category I: Classes 6–8
  • Category II: Classes 9–10

Project Types

  • Research Paper: A theoretical paper solving a scientific problem
  • Working Model: A functional model applying scientific principles

Themes

Environmental Science; Biology and Life Sciences; Physics; Chemistry; Earth and Space Science; Technology and Engineering; Health and Medicine; Mathematics

Submission Guidelines

  • Research Papers: minimum 13 pages; handwritten
  • Working Model reports: minimum 7 pages; handwritten
  • Participants encouraged to document work process with photos and video clips

Presentation

  • 5 minutes per participant, followed by Q&A
  • Special emphasis on feasibility analysis

Assessment Criteria

CriterionWeight
Creativity / Originality and Innovativeness20%
Scientific Research / Technical Skills / Workmanship20%
Quality of Feasibility Analysis20%
Presentation – Effectiveness of Display20%
Project Report20%

Judges

Panel of 3 judges: 1 from LMS, 1 PGT or above from a government organisation, 1 from an external science-based institute.

Action Required

  • All participating schools to submit final list of participants by 25th August 2024
  • Science Teachers and Coordinators guide students in preparation
  • Host School Principal to ensure all logistical arrangements completed by 28th August 2024 along with the event timetable
