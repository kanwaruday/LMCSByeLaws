Subject: LMS Language Literary Fest — Competition Guidelines

LMS Language Literary Fest

For the Inter-School Literary Fest organised on 26th October 2024, the following competitions were held with the guidelines and evaluation parameters below.

Competition Overview

Classes Event Time (min) Language Type Participants per School
3–6 Poem Recitation 2–3 1 English, 1 Hindi Solo 2
3–6 Folk Group Song 4–5 Regional Language Group 5–8
3–6 Spell Bee As per comp. English Solo 1
3–6, 7–10 Painting / Sketching / Art 60 — Solo 6 (3 each category)
7–10 English Group Song 4–5 English Group 5–8
7–10 Literature Quiz As per comp. English In Pairs 2
7–10 News Anchor 3–4 1 Hindi, 1 English Solo 2
7–10 Capture & Create Competition All day English In Pairs 2

Classes 3–6: Competition Guidelines

Poem Recitation

  • The poem must be by an Indian poet
  • A brief about the poet is required
  • Enactment is encouraged
  • 1 student per school per subject language

Folk Group Song

  • 1 group per school
  • Teacher approval required for the song choice

Spell Bee

Multiple levels: Pictionary/Idioms (8 pictures) → Missing Letters (4 words) → Antonyms (4 words) → Rapid Spelling Round (30 seconds) → Hangman (4 words)

Painting / Sketching / Art

  • Topic: Indian Mythology (announced one week in advance)
  • A2 (half chart) size white chart and materials brought by students

Classes 7–10: Competition Guidelines

English Group Song

  • At least 1 instrument must be included (no karaoke)
  • Teacher approval required for song choice
  • Schools must develop Band appearance (clothing, band name, etc.)

Literature Quiz

Sections: Pictionary/Idioms → Guess Author/Character/Movie → Antonyms-Synonyms → Literature Classics → English names for common items → Bollywood movies translated to English. 2 students per school selected at least one week in advance.

Painting

  • Topic: Revival of our Epics
  • A2 chart papers and materials brought by students

News Anchor

  • Students collect news from the past week
  • Cover 10 highlights; 1 case study spoken/read on stage
  • Students encouraged to design a PPT backdrop and dress for their news channel

Capture & Create Competition

  • Students capture the most impactful pictures/videos throughout the event day
  • Produce 1 Reel per school (1 minute) of all activities
  • IT Teacher shares a Google Photos link for uploads
  • Students bring their own devices

Notes

  • For LMS 2 (Classes 11 & 12): students eligible for Painting (3 students), Photography (2 teams), English Group Song (max 1/3 of team)
  • The Editorial Captain serves as team leader of the participating schools
  • Language Teachers are responsible for preparing students
  • Computer Teachers share Backdrops/PPTs with the Host School IT Teacher at least one day in advance

Judgement Parameters

Competition Judgement Parameters (10 marks each)
Poem Recitation Clarity and Expression; Voice Modulation and Pace; Body Language and Confidence
Folk Group Song Authenticity and Cultural Representation; Harmony and Coordination; Stage Presence and Group Dynamics
Painting Competition Creativity and Originality; Relevance to Indian Mythology; Artistic Presentation and Clarity
English Group Song (Seniors) Vocal Quality and Harmony; Instrumental Integration and Balance; Stage Presence and Team Coordination
News Anchor Clarity of Speech and Pronunciation; Presentation and Body Language; Content Delivery and Flow
Capture & Create Number of Likes (+1) & Shares (+10) on Facebook pages
