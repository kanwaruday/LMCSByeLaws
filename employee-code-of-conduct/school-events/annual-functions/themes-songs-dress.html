C. Finalise Themes, Songs, Dress & Event Flow

Theme Approval Process

All teachers bring 2–3 theme options to the Principal. The theme is finalised through voting on the School WhatsApp Group. Parameters to consider:

  • Educational relevance: Align with values like unity, environment, technology, culture, or global harmony (encouraged, not compulsory)
  • Age-appropriateness: Relatable and understandable for all age groups involved
  • Creativity and originality: Fresh, unique ideas over repetitive ones
  • Inclusiveness: Mass participation is compulsory; students rotated during performances so each gets time in the front row
  • Visual appeal: Consider how the theme translates to stage décor, costumes, and acts

Selecting Performances

  • Content appropriateness: Lyrics/dialogue should be meaningful, child-friendly, and culturally sensitive
  • Theme alignment: Performances must reflect the core message of the event/theme if one exists
  • Cultural representation: Include regional, patriotic, and international elements as required
  • Tempo variety: Balanced mix of energetic, emotional, and classical numbers
  • Time limit: Keep performances short and engaging as per the quota

Finalising Event Flow / Sequence

Opening must include:

  • Grand welcome with a song (carnival / flash mob / band display etc.)
  • Devotional Song / Dance / Saraswati Vandana / Ganesh Vandana
  • Lighting of the Lamp

Flow Guidelines:

  • Smooth transitions: Minimal stage reset time; no student clashes
  • Time management: Total event ideally 2.5–3 hours; more entertaining performances distributed evenly
  • Speeches & Prize Distributions: MD's Speech, Principal's Speech, Chief Guest Speech & Prize Distribution, and other prize distributions evenly spread
  • Anchoring script: Professional, concise, with proper cueing and introductions
  • Rehearsal timeline: Final sequence frozen at least 1 week before the event
  • Closing: Vote of thanks by the Principal followed by a group dance performance by teachers

Dress / Costume Guidelines

  • Theme-based: Costumes must reflect the character or essence of the performance
  • Vibrance & Simplicity over extravagance: Avoid costly/extravagant outfits; encourage recycling or rental if possible
  • Colour coordination: No clashes between performances; pick a colour that complements the backdrop colour scheme
  • Gender-sensitive: Ensure comfort, ease of movement, and cultural appropriateness
