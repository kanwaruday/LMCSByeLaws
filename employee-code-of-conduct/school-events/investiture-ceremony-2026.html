Investiture Ceremony 2026

Competitions for selection of school appointees commence on 3rd April 2026. All teachers are expected to impart necessary skills to students for the competitions.

Positions Open for Competition

  • Two Student Representatives for LMS 2 (Class 12)
  • Sports Captain — Class 9/10 (Class 11 for LMS 2)
  • Editorial Captain — Class 9/10 (Class 11 for LMS 2)
  • Band Leader for LMS 2 — Class 9/10
  • Captain – ERST (Class 10 & Class 6)
  • Vice Captain – ERST (Class 9 & Class 5)

Stage Rules

Stage 1

  • All classes — each student is expected to participate
  • Class teachers responsible for conduction and evaluation in their respective classes
  • Deduct 1 mark for each day of delay after the deadline (Class Teachers must enforce strictly)

Stage 2

  • Only students from IC classes are eligible
  • Maximum 16 students per IC class
  • Only Stage 1 shortlisted students participate

Stage 3

  • Only Stage 2 shortlisted students participate
  • Number shortlisted = 2 × number of positions available

Stage 1 Competition Details

Resume Making

  • PW (Classes 3–4): Done in English notebook — includes Name, About Me, School, Skills, Wins, Fun Stuff, and presentation neatness
  • SW (Classes 5–12): Full resume — Header, Objective, Education, Skills, Achievements, Volunteer Experience, Interests, Format
  • Assessment (30 marks): Presentation (10), Academic Achievements (10), Extra-Curricular Achievements (10)

Digital Manifesto (Class 7–12)

  • Designed digitally, printed on A4
  • Must include: bold title, name/photo, vision statement, 3 promises, personal touch, call to action
  • Assessment (30 marks): Clarity of Message (10), Originality & Visual Appeal (10), Alignment with School Values (10)

"Why Me?" Declamation

  • Timing: 2 minutes (up to Class 6), 3 minutes (Class 7+)
  • Focus on vocal delivery, eye contact, confidence, and emotional connection
  • Assessment (60 marks): Delivery, Confidence & Presence (20), Relevance of Content (20), Alignment with School Values (20)

Physical Test

  • Endurance: 20-minute jog (SW: 1 pt/min; PW: 2 pts/min)
  • Strength: Push-ups in 30 seconds (Boys max 10 pts; Girls max 10 pts)
  • Balance (PW only): Stork Stand Test — rank-based points (1st = 10 pts, 2nd = 9 pts, etc.)

Stage 2 Competition Details

Art Project (Class 5–6)

  • Theme: School Decoration; made at home on one chart paper
  • Must be suitable for school display; student explains chart in 1 minute during judging
  • Assessment (60 marks): Explanation of Ideas (20), Creativity & Originality (20), Neatness & Visual Appeal (20)

Extempore (Class 9–12)

  • 1 minute thinking time; 2 minutes speaking time
  • Opinion-based topics (e.g., "Is technology helping or distracting students?")
  • Assessment (60 marks): Content & Ideas (20), Clarity & Fluency (20), Confidence, Body Language & Presentation (20)

Stage 3 Competition Details

Election

  • PW voters: Classes 3–6; SW voters: Classes 7–12
  • Each student casts 4 votes per ballot box for Captains & Vice Captains
  • Points: Highest voted = 10, 2nd = 8, 3rd = 6, 4th = 4, others = 0

Personal Interview (Class 7–12)

  • Conducted by House Moderators + Principal + MDs (optional)
  • Assessment (30 marks): Communication Skills & Confidence (10), Leadership Potential (10), Etiquette & Overall Impression (10)
