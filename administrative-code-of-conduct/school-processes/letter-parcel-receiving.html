Process for Receiving Hard Copy Applications and Letters

Letter & Parcel Receiving

This Office Order establishes the standard 6-step process for receiving and processing all hard copy correspondence — including applications, letters and parcels — received at any LMCS branch.

6-Step Receiving Process

Step 1 — Signature Required

No letter, application or parcel is accepted without the delivery person or applicant obtaining an official acknowledgement. The receiving staff member (typically the front desk or administrative staff) must:

  • Sign the delivery register or acknowledgement slip provided by the sender
  • Note the date and time of receipt
  • Not accept any item without proper sender identification for official correspondence

Step 2 — Receival Register Entry

All received correspondence is immediately logged in the school's Receival Register. The entry must include:

  • Serial number
  • Date and time received
  • Sender's name and organisation
  • Brief description of the contents (e.g., "Job Application – Science Teacher")
  • Name of the staff member who received the item

Step 3 — Principal's Signature on Register

The Receival Register entry must be presented to the Principal for signature. The Principal signs the register to acknowledge that the correspondence has been officially received at the school. This step ensures accountability in the chain of custody.

Step 4 — Principal's Remarks on Application

The Principal reviews the correspondence and writes their initial remarks directly on the application or letter (or on an attached note if the original must be kept clean). Remarks may include:

  • Action required (e.g., "Forward to HR", "File for reference", "Arrange interview")
  • Priority level (urgent / normal)
  • Person responsible for follow-up
  • Deadline for action

Step 5 — Fee Clerk / Admin Staff Files the Application

After the Principal's remarks are recorded, the Fee Clerk or designated administrative staff member files the correspondence in the appropriate folder or file. Filing categories include:

  • Job Applications
  • Parent Correspondence
  • Government / Department Letters
  • Vendor / Supplier Communications
  • Internal Communications

A photocopy is made of all important correspondence before filing, and the copy is forwarded to the relevant department or person for action.

Step 6 — Follow-Up and Closure

The staff member responsible for follow-up (as noted in the Principal's remarks) must action the correspondence within the stipulated timeline. Once actioned:

  • The Receival Register is updated with the action taken and closure date
  • The original file is updated with any response sent
  • The Principal is informed of closure for correspondence that required their initial action
Important: No official correspondence may be discarded without the Principal's written authorisation. All received letters and applications must pass through this process regardless of whether they are deemed relevant.
