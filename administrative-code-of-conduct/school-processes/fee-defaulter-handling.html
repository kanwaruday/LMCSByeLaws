Fee Defaulter Identification and Follow-Up Process

Fee Defaulter Handling

This Office Order establishes the standard process and timelines for identifying, communicating with and managing fee-defaulting families across all LMCS branches.

Roles & Responsibilities

RolePrimary Responsibility
Fee ClerksGenerate monthly defaulter lists, maintain the Parent Communication Register, send email and call reminders
Admin ManagerSupervise the fee follow-up process, escalate to Principal when thresholds are breached, review defaulter list accuracy
PrincipalConduct the monthly review meeting, authorise escalation to MD, approve fee payment plans for genuine hardship cases
Account CoordinatorReconcile fee receipts with defaulter list, prepare branch-level fee collection summary for Head Office, flag unresolved defaults

Monthly Defaulter Timeline

DateActionResponsible
14th of monthGenerate fee defaulter list from school management system. List includes all students with outstanding fees above the branch threshold.Fee Clerk
15th of monthSend email reminders to all defaulting parents from the official branch email ID. Begin phone call follow-up for high-default cases.Fee Clerk
25th of monthUpdate the Parent Communication Register with call logs, responses received and any payment commitments made by parents.Fee Clerk / Admin Manager
26th of monthConduct the monthly defaulter review meeting. Principal reviews the list, Parent Communication Register and decides on escalation for unresolved cases.Principal (with Admin Manager + Fee Clerk)

Branch-Wise Default Threshold Amounts

The following threshold amounts determine when a family is classified as a fee defaulter and the follow-up process is initiated:

BranchThreshold Amount (₹)Notes
LMS 1₹ 8,000Families with outstanding fees exceeding ₹8,000 are classified as defaulters
LMS 2₹ 8,000Families with outstanding fees exceeding ₹8,000 are classified as defaulters
LMS 3₹ 4,000Lower threshold due to lower fee structure; defaulter classification at ₹4,000
LMS 4₹ 7,000Families with outstanding fees exceeding ₹7,000 are classified as defaulters

Escalation Process

  1. Level 1 — Reminder Communication: Email and phone call by Fee Clerk (by 15th)
  2. Level 2 — Admin Manager Review: Cases not resolved after Level 1 are reviewed by Admin Manager who personally calls the parent
  3. Level 3 — Principal Intervention: Cases unresolved after Admin Manager contact are handled directly by the Principal, who may call a parent meeting
  4. Level 4 — MD Escalation: Chronic defaulters (2+ months outstanding above threshold) are escalated to MD with the full case history and the Principal's recommendation

Parent Communication Register

A physical Parent Communication Register must be maintained at each branch. Each entry must record:

  • Student name and class
  • Outstanding amount
  • Date and mode of contact (call / email / in-person)
  • Parent's response
  • Payment commitment (if any) and date promised
  • Outcome of follow-up

The register is reviewed by the Principal at the monthly review meeting and by the Account Coordinator during branch audits.

Fee Payment Plans

In genuine cases of financial hardship, the Principal may approve a fee payment plan allowing the family to pay the outstanding amount in instalments over a maximum of 3 months. Such plans must be:

  • Formally agreed in writing with the parent's signature
  • Reported to MD Academics within 48 hours of approval
  • Monitored monthly to ensure instalments are paid

Fee waiver or write-off requires MD approval and cannot be granted by the Principal alone.
